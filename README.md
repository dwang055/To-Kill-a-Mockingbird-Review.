This is an interactive, self‑contained HTML study app for [To Kill a Mockingbird] by [Harper Lee].
Use it for exam revision, classroom study, or independent reading.

✨ Features
Dark / Light mode – toggle at the top‑right corner; your preference is saved.

Tabbed navigation – quickly jump between:

👥 Characters

🎭 Themes

🔷 Symbols

⏱️ Timeline (Key Moments)

💬 Quotations

📖 Vocabulary

🗺️ Knowledge Map

❓ Quiz

Cross‑linked content – click on any character or theme chip inside a card to jump directly to that item in its respective tab.

Interactive quiz – multiple‑choice questions generated from the quotes and themes. Test yourself and see your score.

Knowledge Map – an at‑a‑glance visualisation of how characters and themes are connected.

Printable – use your browser’s print function to create a paper study guide.

Fully offline – once downloaded, no internet connection is required.

Responsive – works on desktops, tablets, and mobile phones.

🚀 How to Use
Open the file – double‑click the .html file. It works in any modern browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari).

Navigate – click the buttons at the top to switch between sections.

Explore connections – inside each card, look for coloured chips (e.g., a character’s name or a theme). Click them to jump to the related item.

Take the quiz – go to the Quiz tab, answer the multiple‑choice questions, and your score will be shown at the end.

Change theme – click the 🌙 / ☀️ button in the top‑right corner to switch between dark and light mode.

🧠 What’s Inside the App
The app includes detailed, exam‑focused information organised into clear sections:

Section	Content
Characters	Name, role, description, traits, character arc, significance, key quotes, and relationships.
Themes	Definition, in‑depth literary analysis, related characters, and key quotes.
Symbols	Description of the symbol, its meaning, and which theme it connects to.
Timeline	Key moments from the book (chapter by chapter), their significance, and linked themes/characters.
Quotations	Exact quote, speaker, chapter, context, analysis, and links to themes and characters.
Vocabulary	Word, pronunciation, definition, collocations, example quote from the book, analysis, thematic sentence, creative sentence, and links to themes/characters.
Knowledge Map	Automatically generated overview that lists all characters and themes with their connections.
Quiz	Multiple‑choice questions generated from the quotes and themes.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions
Do I need an internet connection to use the app?
No. Once the .html file is saved, you can open it anywhere, even without Wi‑Fi.

Can I print the study notes?
Yes. Use your browser’s Print function (Ctrl+P / Cmd+P). You may wish to use “Print Preview” to adjust margins.

Why doesn’t the Quiz tab show any questions?
The quiz requires at least one quote with a speaker and a listed character, or a theme linked to a character. If the app data does not include these, the quiz cannot generate questions.

The dark/light mode resets every time I open the file – why?
Your preference is saved in your browser’s local storage. If you clear your browser history or use private/incognito mode, the preference will reset.

Can I edit the content of the app?
Yes – you can open the .html file in a plain text editor and modify the embedded JSON data (look for const APP = {...} near the top of the file). However, be careful not to break the JSON syntax. For beginners, it is recommended to use the original data source to regenerate the app.
